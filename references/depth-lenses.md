# Depth Lenses

Use these to add intellectual depth beyond surface-level advice. Pick 1-2 per piece, not all.

## Philosophy Lenses

**Stoicism** — Focus on controllable vs uncontrollable
- Use when: Setbacks, uncertainty, career pressure
- Key move: "What can you actually control here? Your skills, your effort, your response."
- Sources: Marcus Aurelius (Meditations), Epictetus

**Existentialism** — Choice, freedom, identity
- Use when: Career transitions, life direction, quarter-life/mid-life questions
- Key move: "The anxiety isn't about the job. It's about who you become."
- Sources: Sartre, Camus

**Eastern (Dao/Yin-Yang)** — Flow, balance, long-term thinking
- Use when: Strategy, patience, relationship dynamics
- Key move: "Water doesn't fight the rock. It flows around it."
- Sources: Laozi (Dao De Jing), Sunzi

## Sociology Lenses

**Cultural Capital** (Bourdieu) — Hidden advantages from background
- Use when: Explaining "unfair" advantages, networking, education gaps
- Key move: "It's not that they're smarter. They grew up speaking the language of power."

**Group Behavior** (Le Bon, Cialdini) — Herding, social proof, polarization
- Use when: Trend analysis, market prediction, opinion formation
- Key move: "Everyone's saying X, which is exactly why I'm suspicious."

**Generational** — Different cohorts, different survival strategies
- Use when: Workplace culture, consumer behavior, value clashes
- Key move: "Gen Z isn't disloyal. They're rational actors in a different economy."

## Data Techniques

**First-hand** (n=1 but deep):
- Your own experience, interviews, experiments
- Strength: Authenticity, specificity
- "I tried this for 30 days. Here's what actually happened."

**Second-hand** (scaled credibility):
- Academic papers, industry reports, public data
- Always cite source, always acknowledge limitations
- "McKinsey says X — but here's why that might not apply to solo founders."

**Contrast data**: "A is X% higher than B" > "A is Y"
**Personalize data**: "That means if you earn $50K, you're losing..."

## Cross-Domain Analogy

Map unfamiliar → familiar:

| Concept | Analogy |
|---|---|
| Investing | Planting trees (time, patience, compound growth) |
| Career growth | RPG leveling (skill trees, XP, boss fights) |
| Relationships | Bank accounts (deposits, withdrawals, interest) |
| Startups | Expeditions (maps, gear, unknown risks) |
| AI prompting | Talking to a brilliant but literal intern |

**Steps to build good analogies:**
1. Identify the core abstract concept
2. Find a domain your audience knows well
3. Map the structural relationships (not surface features)
4. Acknowledge where the analogy breaks down (builds trust)

## Layered Writing

- **Surface**: Stories, examples, scenes → accessible to everyone
- **Middle**: Methods, frameworks, actionable steps → for practitioners
- **Deep**: Theory, philosophy, first principles → for thinkers

Not every piece needs all three layers. Match depth to platform and audience.
